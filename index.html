About the Project

This project was inspired by the need for a centralized platform for musicians to easily access instruments for rent, sale, or repair. As a musician myself, I wanted to create a space that facilitates the exchange of musical instruments and services within the community.

This is also my Portfolio Project for Holberton School, where I was tasked with developing a full-stack application for my final project.

Rent'y - Bridging the Gap in Tunisia's Music Industry

Introduction

Rent'y is an innovative platform designed to connect musicians and music enthusiasts in Tunisia, providing a marketplace for renting, buying, and selling musical instruments. As a software developer with a passion for music, I saw a unique opportunity to merge my love for the art form with my technical skills. Over two months, I developed this app solo, managing every aspect of the development process. The platform aims to serve musicians and people interested in accessing music instruments digitally.

Personal Story

I’ve been passionate about music since I was 9 years old. Though I didn’t continue pursuing formal music education, I still enjoy playing the guitar and have always felt a connection to music. Growing up in Tunisia, I noticed a gap in the market for a dedicated space where musicians could easily access instruments for buying, selling, or renting. This inspired me to build Rent'y, a platform that combines my love for music with my technical skills. I hope to provide a space that helps musicians access instruments more easily.

Accomplishments

Rent'y’s architecture relies on Flutter for mobile app development, Flask for the API, SQLAlchemy for the ORM, and MySQL for data storage. The flow of data is visualized in the diagram above, illustrating the seamless communication between the mobile app and backend. Major features implemented include:

  • User Authentication: Allowing users to securely sign up, log in, and manage their profiles.
  • Post Creation: Enabling users to create posts for selling or renting their instruments.
  • Search Function: Helping users search for specific instruments.
  • Profile Management: Allowing users to track and manage their posts and information.

Despite the challenges, I successfully implemented these features while maintaining a clean and user-friendly design.

Most Difficult Technical Challenge

As someone new to Flutter, the biggest challenge I faced was learning how to make API calls from the mobile app to the Flask backend. I encountered several issues along the way, but through extensive research and debugging, I finally mastered how to integrate the two parts of the system effectively. This experience taught me a lot about Flutter, API communication, and the importance of persistence in problem-solving.

What I've Learned

Technical Takeaways: I’ve gained valuable experience in integrating Flutter with a Flask backend, using SQLAlchemy for database management, and implementing key app features like authentication and search functionalities.

Personal Growth: Beyond the technical skills, this project has helped me build resilience and improve my problem-solving abilities. Juggling this project while working full-time taught me how to manage time effectively under pressure.

Future Insights: I really enjoyed mobile app development and am excited to pursue this field further in my career. I’ve also developed a deeper understanding of the business side of things. To be a successful developer, I’ve learned that I also need to think critically about business needs and goals.
